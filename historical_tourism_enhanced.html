The Eternal Monument of Love

Built by the fifth Mughal Emperor, Shah Jahan, in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal who died in 1631. The Taj Mahal is widely considered the jewel of Muslim art in India and one of the universally admired masterpieces of the world's heritage.

It took over 20,000 artisans and craftsmen — drawing from Persia, Ottoman Empire, and India — more than 20 years to complete this testament to devotion.
